How car finance works

🚗

Hire Purchase (HP)

You pay a deposit, then fixed monthly payments over an agreed term. Once you've made the final payment, the car is yours. Payments are usually higher than PCP, but you own the car at the end.

  • You own the car after the last payment
  • Fixed monthly payments
  • Good if you want to keep the car long-term
🔄

Personal Contract Purchase (PCP)

Lower monthly payments because you're not paying off the whole car — just the predicted depreciation. At the end you can pay a final "balloon" payment to own it, hand it back, or trade it in.

  • Lower monthly payments than HP
  • Flexible choices at the end of the term
  • Mileage limits usually apply
📅

Personal Loan

You borrow the money from a bank or lender and buy the car outright, then repay the loan. You own the car from day one, and you're a cash buyer as far as the seller is concerned.

  • You own the car immediately
  • No mileage restrictions
  • Rate depends on your credit history

Things to think about

Look at the total cost, not just the monthly

A lower monthly payment over a longer term often means you pay more interest overall. Always check the total amount repayable.

APR is what matters

The Annual Percentage Rate includes interest and most fees, so it's the fairest way to compare deals. A "representative APR" is what most accepted applicants get — yours could differ.

A bigger deposit lowers your payments

Putting more down reduces the amount you borrow, which lowers both your monthly payment and the total interest you pay.

Check you can afford it

Factor in insurance, tax, fuel or charging, servicing and MOTs on top of the monthly finance payment. Never borrow more than you're comfortable repaying.

CarMarket is a marketplace for buying and selling cars. We don't provide, arrange, or recommend finance, and nothing here is financial advice. When you're ready to finance a car, compare options from FCA-authorised lenders and brokers, and read the agreement carefully before signing.
